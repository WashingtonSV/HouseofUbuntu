06 — Usage Rules

Do's &
Don'ts

These rules protect the coherence of the WSV brand universe. Breaking them dilutes the system — following them builds recognition across every touchpoint.

Always do

Use Electric Gold (#F0A500) as the universal connective thread across all entities

Use Barlow Condensed for all display headlines — this is the universal voice

Match the canvas (dark vs. light) to the content type and time of day

Introduce cultural games with their origin story — the learning is part of the experience

Use entity accent colors only for their assigned brand — Sage is HOU, Emerald is MITO, Red is WES

Use Cream (#F2F0EB) for HOU, Warm White (#F5F3EE) for all other entities on light canvas

Allow WSV to remain the quiet parent — it rarely leads in consumer-facing contexts

Maintain generous whitespace — the brands breathe best when not crowded

Never do

Mix entity accent colors across brands — Sage never appears on MITO content; Emerald never on HOU

Use blue in any entity palette — it is deliberately excluded from the universe

Use corporate wellness language, generic fitness brand aesthetics, or cringe wellness jargon

Put a price gate on Streetlights open play — free always, no exceptions

Use purple gradients on white backgrounds or any generic AI-default aesthetic

Position Streetlights NYC as a subsidiary or vertical — it is an HOU event series

Approach sponsors before 3+ documented public sessions with real photos and headcounts

Swap Cream and Warm White between HOU and other entities — the warmth is intentional
